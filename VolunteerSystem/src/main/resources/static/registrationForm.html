DOs for BCI Volunteers

  • Every volunteer should be committed to the policies of BCI.
  • Volunteers must follow the instructions of BCI Head.
  • All the volunteers must fill in their respective time-sheets which includes in-and-out timings and details of work done.
  • Before starting any activity, permission of the concerned doctor should be obtained.
  • It is mandatory for volunteers to wear gloves while handling the animals.
  • Volunteers must be punctual if they have taken responsibility for an event.
  • Volunteers must be clear about the days and timings when they will be available.
  • While joining the organization, volunteers have to go through an interview, orientation program and an induction.
  • It is mandatory for volunteers to carry their ID cards at all times and to be shown on request.
  • Volunteers should follow the escalation clause as given by the CEO/BCI Head.

DON’Ts for BCI Volunteers

  • Any inhumane treatment or harm caused to animals
  • Once assigned to a particular task, volunteers should not drift away from their responsibilities. prior intimation should be given to the BCI office, if they are unable to fulfil commitment.
  • A volunteer should not create any differences between team members or go against the policies of BCI.
  • No volunteer should try to induce any change without the knowledge and concurrence of the Management.
  • Volunteers should not attempt to give instructions to the Vets or the staff at the shelter.
  • The shelter is a hospital with a large number of infected animals and not a zoo and the
  • volunteers should avoid visiting the various sections when they come to the shelter, to prevent the spread of infection and ectoparasites.
  • With the exception of puppies and kittens the volunteers need to avoid the handling of the small mammals and birds such as, rabbits, guinea pigs, parrots etc, unless there is a specific treatment related reason to do so.
  • Doctors are the persons in charge of a particular area without whose permission no one is allowed to enter the kennel.
  • Smoking, chewing tobacco and consumption of alcohol or non-vegetarian food is strictly prohibited within the BCI shelters and in events.
  • Volunteers getting into any altercation with any member of the staff or GB member will be terminated from volunteering with immediate effect.
